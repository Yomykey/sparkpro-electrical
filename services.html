Electrical Services for Your Home

From a single dead socket to a whole-home rewire, here's exactly what we handle — and what it typically involves.

Wiring & Rewiring

Old rubber-insulated cabling, round-pin sockets, or a home extension that's outgrown its circuits — we run new wiring that's safe, fully compliant with UK wiring regulations (BS 7671), and built to handle what your household actually plugs in. This includes new circuits for kitchens and utility rooms, whole-home rewires on older properties, and troubleshooting for sockets or switches that have stopped working.

Every job is inspected and tested before we leave, and we walk you through exactly what was done and why.

Starting at £350 inc. VAT, final quote depends on scope

Consumer Unit Upgrades

If your consumer unit (fuse box) still uses rewireable fuses, trips constantly, or isn't fitted with modern RCD protection, it's likely holding your whole house back — especially if you're adding an EV charger, an electric shower, or extra sockets. We install modern consumer units sized for your home's real load, with clean circuit labelling so you always know what controls what.

Most upgrades are completed in a single day, with power restored the same evening.

Starting at £450 inc. VAT, final quote depends on unit size

We install and wire smart switches, dimmers, and thermostats that need a neutral wire most DIY kits assume you already have, plus dedicated circuits for EV chargers and whole-home battery backups. For new builds and renovations, we can pre-wire for the smart features you're planning before plasterboard goes up.

Bring your own hardware or ask us for a recommendation — either way, we make sure it's wired correctly and safely.

EICR & Safety Inspections

Buying a home, letting out a property, or just haven't had your wiring checked in a decade? We carry out full EICRs (Electrical Installation Condition Reports) — checking your consumer unit, sockets, earthing, and visible wiring against current UK wiring regulations (BS 7671). Landlords are legally required to renew an EICR at least every 5 years; homeowners often need one before a house sale or purchase.

You'll get a full EICR certificate and a plain-English summary, not just a checklist, so you know what's urgent and what can wait.

Starting at £150 flat rate, most homes, inc. VAT
